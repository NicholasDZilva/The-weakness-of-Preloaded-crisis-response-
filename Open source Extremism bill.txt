Draft Bill: Violent Radical Extremism Prevention Act 2026
Part 1 – Preliminary
1. Short Title
This Act may be cited as the Violent Radical Extremism Prevention Act 2026.
2. Commencement
This Act commences on the day after Royal Assent.
3. Purpose and Principles
The purpose of this Act is to prevent, disrupt, and respond to violent radical extremism in Australia by focusing on behaviours, networks, and recruitment activities that pose direct threats to public safety, while preserving citizens’ baseline rights and freedoms.
Principles:
Baseline Preservation: All measures under this Act must respect fundamental legal and civil rights unless directly necessary to prevent violence.
Human Agency Preservation: Interventions shall target actions and behaviours, not beliefs or ideology.
High Coherence: All provisions must be directly aligned with the observable problem of violent radicalisation, ensuring measures are proportionate and evidence-based.
4. Definitions
For the purposes of this Act:
“Violent Radical Extremism” means the planning, preparation, support, or execution of acts intended to cause serious harm, death, or significant property damage in pursuit of ideological, political, or religious objectives.
“Extremist Network” means any organized group or online/offline network that actively recruits, trains, finances, or supports individuals for acts of violent radical extremism.
“Recruitment Activity” means any behaviour intended to persuade, incite, or guide an individual to participate in violent radical activities.
“Intervention Measure” means any legally authorized action taken under this Act to prevent or disrupt violent radical extremism, including monitoring, restrictions, or deradicalisation programs.
“Independent Oversight Body” means a statutory authority established under Part 5 to monitor, review, and audit the implementation of this Act.
Part 2 – Identification and Risk Assessment
5. Risk Assessment Framework
The Minister must develop a national framework for assessing risk of violent radicalisation, including behavioural indicators and credible threat evidence.
The framework must:
Focus on observable behaviours rather than ideology or belief.
Include guidelines for proportional interventions.
Be periodically reviewed by the Independent Oversight Body.
6. Risk Identification Procedures
Law enforcement agencies may identify individuals or networks exhibiting high-risk behaviours that indicate imminent or ongoing violent radicalisation.
Identification requires:
Documented evidence of recruitment, planning, or support for violent acts.
Judicial authorization for any surveillance beyond standard policing powers.
7. Priority Assessment
Individuals or networks assessed as high-risk shall be prioritized for intervention measures.
Interventions must be targeted, proportional, and time-limited, focusing on preventing violence and disrupting radicalisation pathways.
Part 3 – Intervention Measures
8. Preventive Restrictions
Courts may issue preventive orders restricting high-risk individuals from:
Accessing weapons, explosives, or materials that facilitate violence.
Contacting other individuals known to be involved in violent extremist activity.
Accessing extremist forums or communications channels used for recruitment.
Orders must:
Be evidence-based and time-limited.
Include periodic judicial review.
9. Deradicalisation Programs
Voluntary participation in deradicalisation programs shall be encouraged for individuals identified as at-risk.
Programs may include:
Education, counselling, and mentorship.
Employment or vocational pathways to reduce susceptibility to radicalisation.
Outcomes of programs shall be monitored and reported, focusing on risk reduction and reintegration, not ideological conformity.

Part 4 – Law Enforcement Powers and Procedures
10. Targeted Behavioural Enforcement
Law enforcement agencies may take action only against individuals or networks exhibiting behaviours linked to violent radicalisation, including:
Recruitment of others into violent extremism.
Financing or facilitating violent acts.
Planning or attempting violent acts.
Enforcement must not target speech, association, or religious beliefs alone.
11. Surveillance and Investigation
Surveillance of at-risk individuals or networks requires:
Judicial authorization, based on credible evidence of risk.
Periodic review to ensure proportionality.
Covert operations must focus on preventing imminent or ongoing violence, not monitoring ideological adherence.
12. Emergency Interventions
In cases of imminent threat to life or property, law enforcement may act immediately, subject to:
Post-intervention judicial review within 72 hours.
Documentation of specific behaviours or threats justifying action.
Part 5 – Community Engagement and Prevention
13. Community Reporting
Citizens and community organizations may report credible threats of violent radicalisation without fear of legal penalty for speech or association unrelated to violent activity.
Law enforcement must:
Validate reports using evidence-based assessment.
Provide feedback to communities about outcomes while protecting privacy and due process.
14. Education and Public Awareness
The government shall fund educational campaigns to build resilience against radicalisation, including:
Critical thinking, digital literacy, and social media awareness.
Engagement with schools, youth organizations, and local community groups.
Programs must avoid ideological indoctrination and focus on empowering human agency.
Part 6 – Oversight, Transparency, and Accountability
15. Independent Oversight Body
An Independent Oversight Body shall be established to:
Audit risk assessments, interventions, and enforcement actions.
Monitor adherence to baseline rights and proportionality.
Receive complaints regarding misuse or overreach.
16. Reporting Requirements
Annual reports must be submitted to Parliament, including:
Number of interventions, preventive orders, and deradicalisation program participants.
Outcomes measured by reduction in violent extremist incidents.
Review of any civil liberties concerns or complaints.
17. Review and Amendment
The Act shall be reviewed every 3 years to assess effectiveness, proportionality, and alignment with citizens’ problem space.
Parliament may amend measures only after demonstrating coherence with reducing violent radical extremism.
Part 7 – Safeguards for Baseline Rights
18. Protection of Speech and Belief
No person shall be penalized under this Act for lawful expression, religious belief, or political opinion, unless these actions are directly linked to violent radicalisation.
19. Proportionality Requirement
All preventive measures, surveillance, and orders must be:
Evidence-based
Time-limited
Subject to judicial or oversight review
20. Human Agency Safeguards
Individuals targeted by intervention measures shall be:
Fully informed of reasons and rights.
Offered voluntary deradicalisation programs, not compulsory ideological training.
Provided access to appeal or review mechanisms.
Part 8 – Effectiveness Metrics
Success of this Act is measured by:
Reduction in violent radical incidents and successful prevention of planned attacks.
Decrease in recruitment to extremist networks.
Successful reintegration of at-risk individuals through voluntary programs.
Metrics must prioritize observable outcomes over proxy measures, such as online speech content or ideological compliance.
